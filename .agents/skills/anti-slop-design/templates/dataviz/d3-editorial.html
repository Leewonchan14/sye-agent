Renewable energy outpaced fossil fuels for the first time in 2024

A visual essay on the global energy transition

Both sources grew through 2021

Fossil fuel and renewable energy production both climbed steadily through the pandemic recovery, with fossil fuels maintaining a comfortable lead.

Renewables accelerated in 2022

Investment surges in solar and wind pushed renewable output sharply higher while fossil fuel growth plateaued. The gap narrowed from 40% to under 15%.

The crossover happened in late 2024

For the first time, monthly renewable generation exceeded fossil fuel output globally. This single data point marks a structural shift decades in the making.

Data: International Energy Agency (IEA), 2024 World Energy Outlook.
