# Writing Quality Rules

1. **No AI tone markers.** Never use: "In today's...", "Whether you're a... or a...", "Let's dive in", "without further ado", "it's important to note", "at the end of the day".

2. **No corporate buzzwords.** Replace: "leverage" with "use", "utilize" with "use", "synergy" with the actual benefit, "robust" with "strong" or be specific, "seamless" with what actually happens.

3. **Minimal em-dashes.** Maximum one per paragraph. Prefer commas, periods, or restructuring the sentence.

4. **Front-load the point.** First sentence of every paragraph carries the key idea. Don't bury the lead.

5. **Vary sentence length.** Short sentences punch. Longer ones build rhythm and carry nuance when needed. Mix them.

6. **Active voice default.** "We built this" not "This was built by us." Passive voice is acceptable for emphasis shifts, not as a default.

7. **Specific over vague.** "Improved by 34%" not "significantly improved." Numbers beat adjectives.

8. **Read it aloud.** If it sounds like a press release or an AI chatbot, rewrite it.
