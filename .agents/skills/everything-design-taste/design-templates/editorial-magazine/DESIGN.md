# Editorial Magazine Design System

## Color Tokens
```css
:root {
  --color-bg-primary: #FAF9F6;
  --color-bg-secondary: #F0EFEC;
  --color-bg-tertiary: #E8E6E1;

  --color-text-secondary: #666666;
  --color-accent: #C41E3A;
  --color-accent-hover: #A01830;
  --color-border: #D4D2CD;
  --color-success: #2D6A4F;
  --color-error: #C41E3A;

```

## Typography
- Display: "Playfair Display", serif (400, 700, 900)
- Body: "Source Serif 4", serif (400, 500)
- Sans: "Source Sans 3", sans-serif (400, 600) for UI elements
- Large display sizes. Tight leading on headlines. Generous leading on body.

## Spacing
8 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 / 96 / 128

## Radius
0px. Editorial design is sharp.

## Layout
- Multi-column grids (2, 3, 4 columns)
- Pull quotes as full-width interruptions
- Drop caps on article openings
- Asymmetric image placement

## Motion
Minimal. Content-focused. Subtle fade-ins only.

## Personality
The authority of a well-edited magazine. Strong typographic hierarchy carries the entire design. Photography is art-directed, never stock. White space is generous. The reading experience matters more than anything else. Dense when content demands it, airy when the story needs room to breathe.
